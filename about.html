The social and structural condition of families and communities also affect the experiences of the children. Early childhood services work in this greater context and teachers must know how economic pressure, social change, cultural diversity, health issues and crisis events impact children development and wellbeing. Such current Australian policy frameworks as the Early Years Learning Framework Version 2.0 and the National Quality Standard focus on the role of relational pedagogy, inclusive practice and well-established collaboration with families. Such expectations place early childhood educators not simply as teachers, but as pivots who make interpretation of complex social conditions and reaction to them in ways that are developmentally appropriate.

This report is in association to a digital website resource that assists educators to know five key family and community contexts that had been identified in the assessment brief: economic, social, cultural and diversity, health and wellbeing, and crisis and emergency environments. Both of these situations present unique challenges to children but also provide educators with an opportunity to develop resilience, encourage the sense of belonging, and cooperate with the family and community services. The site has easily available practitioner summaries. Those notions are elaborated in this report by means of a profound analysis based on the sociological theory, the recent research, and the present-day Australian policy reaction.

The ecological systems theory proposed by Bronfenbrenner, sociocultural views, and structural approaches to inequality are the basis of the analysis of the influence of wider societal forces on the daily lives of children. These theoretical lenses are useful in understanding the fact that some children feel more vulnerable, whereas other children use supportive factors in their culture, neighbourhood, or family relationships. Recent studies in Australia published since 2020 also facilitate the incorporation of evidence in the report, as the conditions of young children rapidly change, and include the growing costs of living, the growing mobility, the diversification of culture and the consequences of climate-related disasters.

This report will offer early childhood educators a balanced insight into the impact of different contexts in learning and wellbeing of children by connecting theory, research, policy and practice. It also shows how good quality early childhood education may be a stabilising and protective zone to children and families going through complicated situations.
